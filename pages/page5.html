To best prepare for your job interview, you may want to practice questions that you know that your employer will ask you. Questions such as:” what are some strengths or weaknesses?” and “what are some of your hobbies?” are sure to appear in your interview to if you practice these types of questions and come up with creative responses, you will be sure to have a one-up on your employers.

A study conducted by inc.com showed that almost 50% of employers turned potential employees away because of their lack of knowledge of the company that they are applying to. This means that researching the company through websites such as LinkedIn or simply doing a Google search, can greatly increase your chances of having a successful job interview. Also make sure to show your employers that you know about the company in the questions that they may ask you. For example, your employer may ask you “Why are you want to work in this company?” to impress your employer, you would say “I want to be apart of the system that this company has that allows for employees to share ideas without worrying about criticism.

A study on job interviews revealed that employers are more likely to deny a job candidates who does not ask questions about the company than one who does. Even though this may seem like a minor tip, but asking questions can greatly enhance your job interview.
